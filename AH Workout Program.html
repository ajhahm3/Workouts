Day 1 — Push
Mon or Tue · Upper body only · ~55 min
ChestFront Delt TricepsCore
Why This Day Is Built This Way
Opens with flat bench and OHP while your CNS is fresh — compound movements first maximizes motor unit recruitment (Schoenfeld 2010). Incline press and pec deck follow for upper chest volume and a deep pec stretch. Cable lateral raises provide constant tension superior to DBs for lateral delt growth. Triceps close with a superset: pushdown hits the lateral head, overhead extension hits the long head (the largest portion) in a stretched position — both heads in one superset. More ↓

Day 2 — Pull
Wed or Thu · Upper body only · ~55 min
BackBiceps Rear DeltCore
Why This Day Is Built This Way
Pull days counter the damage of desk work — rear delts, lower traps, and external rotators are chronically undertrained in people who sit all day. Research (Cools et al. 2007) links weakness here to shoulder impingement. Weighted pull-ups anchor the session. Face pulls are non-negotiable: rear delt and external rotation in one movement. Hammer curls and preacher curls hit the biceps from two different angles — hammer targets the brachialis and forearm, preacher isolates the bicep in a lengthened position at the bottom. More ↓
A — Compound Pull
Weighted Pull-Up
Strength4 × 5–7Rest 2:30–3 min
⌄
Use a dip belt or hold a DB between your feet. Full dead hang at the bottom every rep — no half reps. Chin fully clears the bar at the top. Log the added weight only.
Saved ✓

Run Day
Fri (recommended) · 30–50 min · Aerobic base + athleticism
CardioAerobicAthletic
Pick Your Run Type
Option A — Easy Aerobic (Zone 2)
30–40 min at conversational pace — you can speak in full sentences. Builds mitochondrial density, fat oxidation, and cardiac output. Best for most weeks.
Option B — Tempo Run
5 min easy → 20 min comfortably hard → 5 min easy. Can say a few words but not full sentences. Builds lactate threshold — the pace you can sustain before it hurts.
Option C — Intervals
5 min easy → 6–8 × (1 min hard / 1 min easy) → 5 min cooldown. Best VO2 max builder. Use sparingly — max once every 2 weeks when lifting 4 days.
Placement
  • Best on Friday — rest day between Day 2 and Saturday's heavy session
  • Avoid running the day before heavy squats (Day 3 Sat) — you want fresh legs
  • Tuesday also works if your schedule shifts
  • Never run the morning of a squat or RDL session
Day 3 — Strength
Saturday (fixed) · Upper + Squat · ~55 min
Heavy PressHeavy Pull SquatCore
Why This Day Is Built This Way
Saturday is your heavy day — lower reps, heavier loads, longer rests. Training at 80–90%+ of 1RM drives neural efficiency and max strength that moderate rep ranges can't replicate (Rhea et al. 2003). Weighted dips and chin-ups are the compound push/pull anchors. The squat is placed mid-session — after upper work when you're warm but not fatigued. Alternate front and back squat week to week. Core closes with loaded carries and weighted plank — anti-lateral flexion is the most neglected core pattern in most athletes. More ↓
A — Compound Strength (Upper)
Dips
Strength4 × 8–10Rest 2:30 min
⌄
Bodyweight. Upper arms go below parallel at the bottom — don't cut the ROM short. Slight forward lean for chest emphasis. Full extension at top. Control the descent completely. 8–10 clean reps per set. If that becomes easy across all 4 sets, add a plate.
Bodyweight — no weight to log.
Chin-Ups
Strength4 × 6–8Rest 2:30 min
⌄
Bodyweight. Supinated grip (palms facing you) — maximizes both lat and bicep engagement. Full dead hang at the bottom every rep — no half reps. Chin clears the bar at the top. Slow, controlled descent.
Bodyweight — no weight to log.

Day 4 — Hypertrophy
Sunday (fixed) · Upper + RDL · ~55 min
ShouldersArms Upper BackRDLCore
Why This Day Is Built This Way
Sunday is high-volume, lower load — after Saturday's heavy session. Every muscle gets its second weekly frequency hit here for arms, shoulders, and upper back (Ralston et al. 2017). RDL is placed first while you're fresh — it's the second hamstring/posterior chain stimulus of the week and supports running stride. Z-Press on the floor eliminates all leg drive, making it a pure deltoid press. Triceps superset mirrors Day 1 to accumulate total weekly volume across both tricep heads. Core closes with rotational power — a direct callback to your lacrosse movement patterns. More ↓

Program Notes

Come back next session and fill in the next row. You'll always be able to see your last 4 sessions at a glance. Data is stored in your browser and stays there every time you open this page.

Progressive Overload
  • Hit the top of the rep range on all sets before adding load
  • Upper body: add 5 lbs. Lower body: add 10 lbs
  • If form breaks down, the weight is too heavy — drop it
  • Every 8–10 weeks: deload week at ~50–60% normal volume
Warm-Up (Every Session)
  • Band pull-aparts — 2 × 15, every session, no exceptions
  • Shoulder external rotation with band — 2 × 15 per side
  • Ramp-up sets on first compound lift: ~50% × 8, then ~75% × 4
  • Squat days: add hip flexor stretch + bodyweight squat × 10
Sat + Sun Back-to-Back
  • Day 3 (Sat): strength-biased — heavy, low volume, long rests. CNS demand high, total rep count low
  • Day 4 (Sun): hypertrophy-biased — lighter loads, higher reps, different fatigue profile — fully manageable
  • Prioritize 7–9 hrs sleep Friday night going into the heavy Saturday session
Shoulder Health
  • Face pulls on Day 2 are non-negotiable — this is your rotator cuff maintenance
  • Push:pull volume ratio across the week is ~1:1 — protective by design
  • Pressing with elbows at 90° is the #1 cause of impingement — keep at ~70°
Nutrition & Recovery
  • Protein: 0.7–1g per lb of bodyweight daily — non-negotiable for hypertrophy
  • On run days: prioritize carbs — glycogen drives endurance performance
  • Sleep is where muscle is actually built — 7–9 hrs is not optional, it IS the program
